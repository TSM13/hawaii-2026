Traveling with Little Ones

Tips for the 2-year-old and the 1-year-old 🧒

Practical notes for making this trip smooth with young kids in the group. General guidance — adjust to your family and confirm details as you plan.

🏖️ Calm-water beaches near each stay

  • Big Island: Kahaluʻu Beach Park is tide-protected and great for wading/snorkel; the Mauna Lani / Waikoloa resort beaches (Anaehoʻomalu Bay) are gentle and shady.
  • Kauai (Poipu): Poipu Beach Park has a shallow, semi-protected keiki (child) pool area — ideal for toddlers and easy from the house.
  • Maui (Kihei): Kamaʻole I–III beach parks are right by the house with lifeguards, grass, restrooms, and calmer morning water.
  • Mornings are usually calmest and least crowded — best window for the little ones.

🧳 Gear & rentals

  • Car seats: reserve with the rental car or rent from a baby-gear service — 2 needed (the 2-yr-old + 1-yr-old). (See the trip checklist reminder.)
  • Cribs / pack-n-plays, high chairs, beach gear, strollers: baby-gear rental companies (e.g. BabyQuip) deliver to Airbnbs on all three islands — often cheaper/easier than flying with it all.
  • A soft baby carrier beats a stroller on sand, trails, and the Nā Pali/helicopter days.
  • Pack a small sound machine / familiar blanket to keep naps consistent across 3 houses.

☀️ Sun & safety for the kids

  • Toddlers burn fast — lean on shade (umbrella, UV tent, hats, rash guards/long sleeves) more than sunscreen alone.
  • Use reef-safe mineral sunscreen (required in Hawaiʻi) for the older kids and adults.
  • Reef/water shoes protect little feet on lava rock and reef (Kahaluʻu, tide pools).
  • Hydration + electrolytes for everyone — it's hotter and sunnier than it feels with the trade winds.

😴 Nap-friendly scheduling

  • Easiest days (short drives / home base): Molokini morning (~15 min from Kihei), Maui & Kauai beach days, Mauna Kea is relatively close (~1 hr).
  • Long / winding drives — plan around naps, pack snacks, expect motion sickness: Road to Hana, Haleakalā (cold summit), Waimea Canyon, Hanalei, and optional Volcanoes NP.
  • Consider tag-teaming: some adults do a big drive/tour while others stay back with the youngest at the beach or house.
  • The manta ray, Haleakalā, and Mauna Kea nights run late — decide which little ones (if any) join.

🛒 Feeding & groceries

  • Do a grocery run on each arrival day for diapers/pull-ups, milk, toddler snacks, and easy meals — grocery links are on each stay card on the Home page.
  • Big Island: Foodland Farms at the Shops at Mauna Lani. Kauai: markets in Koloa/Poipu. Maui: Kihei has several (Foodland, Times, Safeway).
  • Airbnb kitchens make it easy to cook simple kid meals and keep nap/bedtime routines.
  • Most casual restaurants have high chairs and keiki menus — see Dining & Reservations.

✈️ Flights & transitions

  • Three inter-island flights (Oct 7, 12) plus the long haul home (Oct 17) — build buffer time for car-seat installs, gear, and rental returns.
  • A drink, snack, or pacifier on takeoff & landing helps little ears.
  • Keep essentials (diapers, wipes, change of clothes, snacks) in one carry-on per child.

These are general tips gathered for this trip — always confirm current rules, rentals, and conditions before you rely on them.
